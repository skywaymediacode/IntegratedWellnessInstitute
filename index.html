01 About IWI

We treat resilience like the professional skill it is.

The Integrated Wellness Institute was founded by two doctorate-level clinicians with one shared conviction: resilience is a capability, not a trait. Like any capability, it can be trained, practiced, and reinforced.

That conviction shapes everything we build. Our programs are grounded in evidence-based practices, designed for the operational realities of first responders, and engineered for adoption — not just delivery. We pair every course with a companion book so the work continues long after the training room.

Our mission: equip the people who carry the weight of public safety, public health, and public service with tools that hold when the work demands the most of them.

03 The Methodology

The PEACE Resilience Model.

A resilience model built specifically for first responders — and uniquely aligned with trauma treatment. Five evidence-based components build resilience as a daily, repeatable practice, refined through years of working directly with those who operate under chronic stress and traumatic exposure. Several distinct elements set the PEACE Model apart from conventional resilience training.

01 P

Psychoeducation on Trauma

Identify the impact of trauma on the mind and body — and why understanding the mechanism is the first step toward managing it.

02 E

Expand Awareness

Build emotional and environmental awareness so personnel can read their own state — and the situation — before either escalates.

03 A

Activate Healthy Habits

Practical routines that meet needs across every level of wellbeing: physical, cognitive, emotional, and relational.

04 C

Coping with Acute Stress

Evidence-based techniques personnel can use in the moment, on scene, on shift — not just in a quiet room afterward.

05 E

Establish a Plan

An individualized plan personnel actually return to — for building resilience and maintaining wellbeing through a long career.

Why it matters.

Today's organizations face increasing demands related to workforce wellness, employee engagement, recruitment, retention, leadership development, and organizational culture. Investing in resilience, wellness, and peer support helps create environments where individuals and teams can perform at their best while navigating the challenges of high-demand professions.

  • Organizational health and long-term success

06 The Founders

Doctorate-level clinicians.
Practitioner credibility.

Together, Dr. Stewart-Spencer and Dr. Agliata combined clinical expertise, resilience education, organizational strategy, and practical implementation to develop the PEACE Model and create scalable programs that support trauma-exposed professionals nationwide through the Integrated Wellness Institute. Their shared mission and collaborative work have allowed them to develop innovative trainings, publications, certifications, and organizational initiatives designed to create meaningful, sustainable, and real-world impact.

S

Dr. Sarah Stewart-Spencer

PhD · LMHC · NCC · CFRP

Dr. Sarah Stewart-Spencer is the founder of Integrated Wellness Institute and specializes in trauma and emergency mental health care, resilience education, program development, operational strategy, and scalable training design. Her work focuses on translating complex behavioral health and wellness concepts into engaging, actionable programs that strengthen both individual well-being and organizational culture. Through collaborative leadership and shared vision, she helped transform the foundational concepts of the Mastering Resiliency series into the PEACE Model used today, contributing significantly to the development of scalable training frameworks and organizational applications that organizations can successfully implement and sustain.

A

Dr. Allison Kanter-Agliata

PhD · BCC · CFRP

Dr. Allison Kanter Agliata is a licensed clinical psychologist, board-certified executive coach, speaker, and author specializing in resilience, trauma exposure, peer support, critical incident response, and organizational wellness. Through years of direct clinical and organizational work with first responders, military members, leadership teams, and other high-demand professionals, she developed the foundational concepts behind the Mastering Resiliency series. As her clinical practice and organizational work rapidly expanded, she partnered with Dr. Stewart-Spencer and Integrated Wellness Institute to broaden the reach of these concepts through scalable educational programs, trainings, certifications, and organizational initiatives. Her work emphasizes practical resilience strategies, sustainable performance, and real-world application within high-stress professions.
